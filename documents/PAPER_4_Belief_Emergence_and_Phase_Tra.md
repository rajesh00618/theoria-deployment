## Abstract

We study the emergence of collective beliefs in multi-agent systems. Through systematic parameter sweeps, we discover a phase transition between consensus and fragmentation at noise* = 0.075. The transition is topology-invariant and scale-invariant but depends on belief representation. Real-world networks show the same transition at noise* = 0.30. The mechanism is competitive dynamics between consensus and fragmentation forces.
