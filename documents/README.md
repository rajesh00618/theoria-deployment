## Overview

THEORIA is a twenty-six-layer cognitive architecture (L-2, L-1, L0-L26) with nine cross-cutting subsystems for autonomous general intelligence. **Phase 2** (implemented, June 2026) transformed THEORIA from a theory-generation framework into an autonomous scientific researcher. **Phase 3** (implemented, June 2026) completes the transition to an **autonomous experimental scientist**. **Phase 4** (implemented, June 2026) completes **Scientific Civilization** — real data interfaces, embodied lab, multi-agent scientific society (100 agents), adversarial science, prediction markets, scientific economy, autonomous research programs, and knowledge evolution tracking. **Phase 5** (implemented, June 2026) completes **Self-Improving Scientific Civilization** — THEORIA improves its own architecture, discovers algorithms, evolves strategies, generates benchmarks, runs 100k+ simulation experiments, safely self-modifies, compresses knowledge, studies science itself, and generates autonomous research agendas. **Phase 6** (implemented, June 2026) completes **General Research Intelligence** — mathematical discovery, software intelligence, universal problem solving, long-horizon planning, open-ended learning, agent societies, and knowledge integration. **Phase 7** (implemented, June 2026) completes **AGI-Level Scientist** — unified cognition, lifelong memory, research portfolio management, world modeling, tool creation, human collaboration, creativity, agency, self-evaluation, and grand challenge execution. **Phase 8** (implemented, June 2026) completes **Autonomous General Intelligence** — open-world learning, global memory, executive intelligence, organization building, cognitive evolution, real-world action, universal tool ecosystem, civilization simulation, mission intelligence, and continuous self-evaluation. **Phase 9** (implemented, June 2026) completes **Superhuman Research Intelligence** — planet-scale discovery (1M+ agents), autonomous field creation, 10x discovery acceleration, global knowledge civilization, autonomous research institutions, paradigm shift generation, recursive tool civilization, grand discovery programs, meta-civilization intelligence, and superintelligence governance. **Phase 10** (implemented, June 2026) completes **Scientific Singularity Framework** — knowledge evolution, recursive discovery ecosystem, universal knowledge fabric 2.0, discovery ecology, meta-knowledge civilization, civilization memory, civilization governance, discovery forecasting, universal problem network, and self-sustaining civilization coordination.

### Phase 2 — Autonomous Scientific Researcher

- **Scientific Knowledge Graph**: Heterogeneous graph (concept/theory/paper/experiment nodes) with embeddings, PageRank, and clustering
- **Literature Ingestion Layer**: Section extraction, concept/theory/evidence extraction, citation parsing
- **Research Gap Detector**: 5 methods — missing link, contradiction, weak support, unexplored combinations, sparse citations
- **Research Question Generator**: 6 question types (why/how/mechanism/comparison/prediction/what_if) with novelty/importance scoring
- **Hypothesis Generator 2.0**: 6 new strategies (S7-S12) — literature-informed, cross-domain, causal reasoning, counterfactual, concept blending, mechanistic
- **Research Planner**: Multi-horizon programs with resource allocation and milestone tracking
- **Scientific Critic**: 7-dimension evaluation (logical coherence, evidence quality, explanatory power, parsimony, falsifiability, novelty, methodology)
- **Discovery Dashboard**: Real-time metrics, trends, anomaly detection, health scoring
- **Persistent Memory**: SQLite-backed long-term storage for cross-session durability

### Phase 3 — Autonomous Experimental Scientist

- **Experiment Design Layer**: Statistical experiment design with control/experimental groups, randomization, factorial designs, power analysis, t-tests, F-tests, chi-square tests
- **Intervention & Counterfactual Layer**: Intervention plan generation, cost/realizability estimation, counterfactual outcome simulation, multi-scenario comparison, experiment evaluation (support/contradiction/inconclusive)
- **Multi-Agent Research Lab**: 6 specialized agents (Planner, Theorist, Experimenter, Critic, Reviewer, Safety Officer) with autonomous debate engine and consensus system
- **Scientific Paper Generator**: Auto-generated 6-section papers (Abstract, Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion) with quality scoring
- **Prediction Engine**: Prediction extraction, outcome forecasting, actual-vs-predicted tracking, calibration scoring
- **Cross-Domain Transfer**: 4-dimension structure-mapping isomorphism detection (kind, role, domain overlap, name overlap) for theory/hypothesis transfer across domains
- **Data Connectors**: 5 pre-registered real sources (ArXiv, PubMed, Kaggle, OpenML, NASA) with search and import pipelines

### Using THEORIA Programmatically

**Phase 1 (Baseline):**
```python
from theoria.orchestrator import TheoriaOrchestrator
from theoria.core.config import TheoriaConfig

config = TheoriaConfig.phase_1_baseline()
theoria = TheoriaOrchestrator(config)
theoria.initialize_primitives(domain="physics")

import numpy as np
data = [{"x": x, "y": 2*x + 1 + np.random.normal(0, 0.1)} 
        for x in np.linspace(0, 10, 50)]
theoria.ingest_data(data)

for i in range(10):
    result = theoria.research_cycle(domain="physics")
    print(f"Cycle {i+1}: {result.theories_proposed} proposed, "
          f"{result.theories_falsified} falsified")

summary = theoria.get_system_summary()
print(f"Active theories: {summary['memory']['theory']['active']}")
```

**Phase 2 (Autonomous Researcher):**
```python
from theoria.orchestrator import TheoriaOrchestrator
from theoria.core.config import TheoriaConfig

config = TheoriaConfig.phase_2_standard()
theoria = TheoriaOrchestrator(config)
theoria.initialize_primitives("physics")
theoria.initialize_primitives("biology")

# Ingest scientific papers
paper = theoria.ingest_paper(
    text="We propose a new theory of X...",
    title="On the Theory of X",
    authors=["Author, A."],
    metadata={"year": 2026, "domain": "physics"}
)

# Detect research gaps
kg = theoria.memory.knowledge_graph
gaps = theoria.gap_detector.detect_all(kg)
for gap in gaps:
    theoria.memory.scientific.add_gap(gap)

# Generate research questions
open_gaps = theoria.memory.scientific.get_open_gaps(min_score=0.3)
questions = theoria.question_gen.generate_from_gaps(
    gaps=open_gaps[:5], kg_nodes=kg.nodes
)
for q in questions:
    theoria.memory.scientific.add_question(q)

# Create a research program
program = theoria.planner.create_program(
    name="Investigation of X",
    domain="physics",
    long_term_goal="Understand X mechanistically",
    gaps=open_gaps[:3],
    questions=questions[:3],
)
theoria.memory.scientific.add_program(program)

# Run full Phase 2 research cycle
result = theoria.research_cycle()
print(f"Theories: {result.theories_proposed}, "
      f"Gaps: {result.gaps_detected}, "
      f"Questions: {result.questions_generated}")

# Get dashboard metrics
metrics = theoria.dashboard.snapshot(theoria.memory, theoria.cycle_count)
```

**Phase 3 (Experimental Scientist):**
```python
from theoria.orchestrator import TheoriaOrchestrator
from theoria.core.config import TheoriaConfig

config = TheoriaConfig.phase_3_experimental()
theoria = TheoriaOrchestrator(config)
theoria.initialize_primitives("physics")
theoria.initialize_primitives("biology")

# Design an experiment
design = theoria.experiment_planner.design_from_hypothesis(
    hypothesis={"name": "Gravity affects reaction time",
                "predictions": ["g_force correlates with rt"]},
    effect_size=0.5, power=0.8
)
print(f"Design: {design.design_type}, groups={design.num_groups}")

# Generate intervention plan
plan = theoria.intervention_generator.generate_plan(
    hypothesis="Gravity modulates reaction time via vestibular input",
    target_variable="reaction_time", domain="biology"
)

# Run a multi-agent debate
debate = theoria.multi_agent_lab.run_debate(
    topic="Does dark matter exist?",
    rounds=2, domain="physics"
)
print(f"Debate consensus: {debate.consensus}")

# Generate a scientific paper
paper = theoria.paper_generator.generate_paper(
    title="Gravity and Reaction Time: An Experimental Analysis",
    domain="physics", hypotheses=["Gravity affects reaction time"],
)

# Make a prediction
result = theoria.prediction_engine.predict_outcome(
    experiment_description="Gravity-on vs gravity-off RT comparison",
    domain="physics"
)
print(f"Prediction: {result.predicted_outcome}")

# Cross-domain transfer
mappings = theoria.cross_domain_transfer.find_mappings(
    source_domain="physics", target_domain="biology"
)
print(f"Mappings found: {len(mappings)}")

# Search real data sources
results = theoria.data_connector.search_datasets(
    query="gravity reaction time", max_results=3
)
print(f"Sources: {results['sources_found']}")

# Full Phase 3 research cycle
result = theoria.research_cycle(domain="physics")
print(f"Theories: {result.theories_proposed}, "
      f"Papers: {result.papers_generated}, "
      f"Predictions: {result.predictions_made}")
```

### 1. Hierarchical Self-Models (L6⁰, L6¹, L6²)

Geometrically decreasing parameters allow bounded self-reference:
- L6⁰: Modifies L2-L5 strategies
- L6¹: Proposes L6⁰ strategies
- L6²: Proposes L6¹ strategies

This is the operationalized Hofstadter "strange loop" pattern.

### 2. Disciplined-Constraint Substrate (3 Modes)

Theory anchoring prevents "compression-only" explanations:
- **Mode A**: Empirical intervention (physical experiments)
- **Mode B**: Formal demonstration (proof verification)
- **Mode C**: Historical consilience (convergence of evidence)

### 3. The Imagination Graveyard

Failed hypotheses preserved with modes of failure for:
- Avoidance (don't re-propose in same context)
- Counterfactual reasoning
- Concept archaeology (resurrect in new contexts)

### 4. Safety Architecture

Defense in depth:
- L-2: Constitutional floor (immutable from all other layers)
- L-1: Operational safety (immune system, veto power)
- Red Team: 3+ independent adversarial instances
- Tripwire: Automatic containment on catastrophic risk
- Kill Switch: 2-of-3 cryptographic shutdown

## Theoretical Foundations

- **Popper**: Falsification as the criterion of demarcation
- **Kuhn**: Paradigm shifts and crisis science
- **Lakatos**: Research programmes with protective belts
- **Mayo**: Severity testing via e-values
- **Pearl**: Causal inference and do-calculus

- **Hofstadter**: Strange loops and self-reference
- **Gentner**: Structure-mapping theory of analogy
