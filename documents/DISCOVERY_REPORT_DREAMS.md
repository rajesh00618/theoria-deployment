## Winning Hypothesis

### Dreams as Predictive Coding Error

Dreams are the brain's way of reporting prediction errors that accumulated during the day, highlighting mismatches between expected and actual outcomes that need to be updated.

### Mechanism

The brain constantly predicts sensory input. During waking, prediction errors are suppressed to maintain focus. During sleep, these suppressed errors are replayed and amplified, producing the subjective experience of dreams. The dream content IS the prediction error signal.

### Testable Predictions

1. Dreams should contain more unexpected events than waking life
1. Days with more surprises should produce more vivid dreams
1. Dreams should help update predictive models (improve future predictions)
1. People who are more surprised during the day should have more memorable dreams
