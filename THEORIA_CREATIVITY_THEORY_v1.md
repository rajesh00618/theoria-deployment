## 4. Winning Hypothesis: Creativity as Optimal Noise

### Description

Creativity requires an optimal level of neural noise. Too little noise produces stagnant, repetitive thinking. Too much noise produces random, incoherent output. Optimal noise enables structured exploration of idea space.

### Mechanism

Neural noise creates random perturbations in thought. At optimal levels, these perturbations explore novel regions of idea space while maintaining enough structure to be meaningful. This is the same Optimal Diversity Principle discovered in RP-001.

### Predictions

1. Moderate neural noise correlates with creativity
1. Too much or too little noise reduces creativity
1. Creativity follows the Optimal Diversity curve
1. Noise-induced exploration produces novel combinations
